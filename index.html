My Story

My software development journey started when I built my first website as a child — a fan page for the Nickelodeon show Rugrats — with the help of various online tutorials and by scanning the source of other sites I admired. Between other interests like digital art and photography, my interest in software followed me into high school, where I helped maintain the district website and built an application to manage a class-wide game of Assassin using PHP and MySQL.

After college, my journey took a detour while I pursued a career in writing. Although it was ultimately not the path for me, I still cherish the education it gave me. Writing isn't just about stringing together grammatically correct sentences, after all: it's about communication, empathy, and organizing complex ideas in a way that's coherent and engaging to others, all of which have proven to be invaluable skills as a developer.

During that period, I never stopped coding. Mostly for myself at first — I enjoyed the problem solving aspect and how easily I could automate day-to-day tasks, e.g. comparing the price of books across different vendors or generating a daily news feed and sending it to my Kindle — but gradually I also started taking on more freelance work and leveling my skills along the way. This eventually inspired me to switching over to coding full time, and I've been loving what I do ever since!
